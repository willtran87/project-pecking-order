# Pecking Order

A darkly comic 3D management game about chickens processing insurance claims inside a corporate office. Hens peck through work and lay eggs as deliverables, roosters manage their performance, and the farmer presents the results as evidence of his leadership.

## Current Vertical Slice

The first playable foundation includes:

- A complete five-shift probation campaign with New/Continue intake, automatic checkpoints, cumulative score, five management ranks, early termination, and a final pass/fail review
- An uncapped Senior Roost career after successful probation: three-shift quarters, four-quarter annual reviews, lifetime Roost Marks and promotion titles, exact pass/fail safeguards, a mandatory capital-policy tradeoff before every quarter, and optional mark-funded Career Sponsorships
- A causal shift receipt that shows the exact score change and its five contributing ledgers, plus one factual hen highlight from the completed shift
- A persistent, skippable First Clutch induction with a paused floor orientation that teaches one real hen from inspection through specialty routing, check-in, Priority Peck, egg grading, and farmer presentation

- Six persistent hen identities with career profiles, XP, titles, manager trust, grievances, employment records, and permanent secondary-lane credentials; new probation files begin with four staffed perches
- Incoming insurance claims

- Sound, cracked, and golden eggs

## Management Loop

Every shift opens with three morning-policy cards. The player chooses throughput, shell assurance, or sustainable flock care, reviews the complete tradeoff, and explicitly authorizes it. A new file holds Day 1 at a paused floor orientation until the player inspects a hen and chooses 1×; later briefings resume normally. At 11:00 AM and 2:00 PM, satirical office incidents auto-pause the shift and require a response; each option exposes its cost before it can be confirmed. The top HUD keeps the live egg quota, clean-clutch streak, and active policy visible throughout the shift. Sound and golden eggs extend the streak and add a capped value bonus; a cracked egg resets it.

On a fresh file, the optional First Clutch coach locks onto the first inspected hen and follows only authoritative actions. It remembers valid actions completed out of order, permits wrong routing and missed Peck windows, survives Continue, and retires permanently after the target hen's assisted egg reaches the farmer or the player chooses Skip. The compact card never pauses production or replaces the selected-hen dossier.

The live queue strip separates Nest Damage, Predator Loss, and Appeals work. Selecting a hen opens her assignment dossier with specialty, current file, deadline, value, and crack risk. AUTO dispatch favors specialty and urgency; manual routing can rescue an overdue tray at the cost of a slower, riskier mismatch. Failed files return as next-shift rework, so today's shortcuts remain visible in tomorrow's workload.

When a seated hen's active claim reaches the gold section of its progress bar, the dossier exposes a Priority Peck. Pressing `E` performs a connected three-peck animation, advances only that exact claim, and changes its real quality risk. Perfect and strong timing build a chain that pays a small clean-credit bonus only if the finished egg survives grading. The limit of three assists per shift keeps it a deliberate intervention instead of another idle multiplier.

The same dossier also exposes each hen's career title, XP, work profile, trust, and grievance. Once per shift, management can share basket credit, fund perch-side coaching, or authorize a stretch clutch for one selected hen. Profiles disclose which choice is the strongest fit. Credit improves morale and trust but costs money and farmer favor; coaching trades current speed for safer work and advancement; pressure raises output now while creating persistent quality and relationship debt. Policies, incident responses, Feed Parties, and overtime also shape the flock's memory.

On Days 2 and 4, that memory can produce a named Flock Petition in place of the second structural incident. Specialty mismatch, dangerous pace, and prior credit allocation become evidence instead of hidden modifiers. Management can sign an exact next-shift compact, buy a temporary scoop-of-feed concession, or deny and monitor. Signed promises are tested once against the authoritative closing ledger; releasing the sponsor or breaking the wording creates a breach receipt. At sufficient flock unity, denial or breach schedules one shift of work-to-rule: 18% less throughput in exchange for six points of shell safety. Flockwatch and the sponsor's dossier keep the obligation visible.

New probation files open with four occupied workstations and two applicants. During the farmer-review planning window, Flockwatch shows each candidate's specialty, work profile, career rank, one-time hire cost, and recurring wage. Management can authorize the fifth and sixth physical perches, hire into a vacant desk, or release an idle hen to pasture with a real separation package and lasting flock consequences. Hen identities and career histories survive employment changes instead of resetting into interchangeable units.

Completed eggs pause at the physical sorter for grading. The machine issues a quality/value receipt, then the egg continues along the presentation rail; its value appears in the Feed Fund only when it reaches the farmer. The same real egg settles into the basket or cart, bounces once, and remains there with its sound, golden, or cracked treatment until the next shift. This makes the satire mechanically legible: the hen creates the value, but the farmer's collection is the credited event.

The Flockwatch Ledger combines the live roster and operating reserve with three permanent requisitions whose costs rise across five levels:

- **Beak-Friendly Keycaps:** add visible keycaps to every workstation and increase peckwork speed by 8% per level.
- **Shell Integrity Lamp:** adds and brightens a physical QA desk lamp while reducing crack risk by 2.5 percentage points per level.
- **Ergonomic Nest Pad:** adds a visible chair cushion and reduces stress and fatigue gain by 10% per level.

Each shift allows one $20 Feed Party. Funding it improves morale and stress, pauses production while every employed hen attends the trough, then restores the prior simulation speed when they return. Overtime provides 22% more throughput but increases fatigue, stress, morale loss, and crack risk; it resets at the next shift.

At 5:00 PM the simulation pauses behind a full-screen farmer review. The report compares eggs against the target, records cracked and golden output, names the shift policy and resolved incidents, applies quota and quality bonuses, then itemizes feed, payroll, facility overhead, paid wages, any compact fulfillment or breach, and completed or scheduled work-to-rule before announcing the next quota. Obligations are reserved in advance, so discretionary spending cannot erase the flock's operating costs.

The farmer review now feeds a five-shift probation file. Each day has three explicit orders in Flockwatch, and the second review screen converts the shift into a cumulative probation score, rank, welfare average, coop-obedience average, and farmer-favor average. After shift two, one permanent specialization must be selected before the third briefing. Passing requires a score of at least 60 plus minimum flock, compliance, favor, and shell-quality safeguards; a collapsed score can end probation early. A passing file can continue into an uncapped Senior Roost, while a failed file can be retried.

Senior Roost is a recurring career rather than a renamed sixth probation day. Every quarter lasts three shifts and begins with one irreversible capital policy: concentrate $12 in Merit Grants for the top hen, spend $24 on a flock-wide dividend, or book $24 of future harvest now while increasing quota pressure and relationship debt. Output, shell integrity, queues, welfare, compliance, farmer favor, and solvency produce an exact 100-point quarter score and up to three lifetime Roost Marks. At each closed-quarter gate, management may bank those marks or invest three marks plus $12 protected Feed Fund in one Accredited Layer's Career Sponsorship. Her next shift with real claim progress runs 15% slower for training; afterward she permanently receives specialist speed and shell-risk treatment in one alternate lane, a torso credential, and a $1 daily wage increase. AUTO dispatch still prefers her primary lane, so the credential expands manual routing choices instead of removing them. Four quarters close an annual review; passing awards bonus marks and raises next year's baseline quota by one, while failure opens a performance-improvement year with a two-egg increase and reduced farmer favor. Either result can continue into another uncapped year and named promotion tier.

That second review now opens with an exact score receipt instead of an unexplained total. Probation orders, daily clutch, shell quality, queue control, and flock safeguards each show their signed contribution; score caps and the shift-two specialization appear as separate reconciliation lines when applicable. Beside the filed management memo, a factual hen file preserves one character-scale moment from the shift: pressured cracked work, a golden deliverable, dangerous strain, invisible labor, or the closing ledger leader. The simulation freezes that story before daily stats and overnight strain reset, so its identity, output, relationships, and satirical copy all describe the same completed shift.

After Day 4, the farmer files a Flock Restructuring order against the bottom hen in the frozen Pecking Order. The dossier includes the ranking and the context its score omitted: specialty mismatch, career history, trust, grievance, strain, and management pressure. The player can nominate her as the variance, pay to redeploy her into her specialty, or contest the ranking with the whole flock. That choice is persisted and names the campaign ending: Farmer's Favorite, Benevolent Rooster, or The Flock Has a Voice.

Campaign progress is saved as versioned JSON after meaningful management actions, including policy and incident resolutions, petitions, compacts, work-to-rule, personnel check-ins, routing changes, hiring, releases, perch authorizations, purchases, Feed Parties, overtime changes, Priority Pecks, credited eggs, restructuring, completed shifts, Senior capital filings, Career Sponsorships, scored quarters, and annual transitions. `Continue` restores the immutable probation ledger, the separate Senior career ledger, pending or completed cross-training, and the exact live office checkpoint; a verified backup is used if the primary save is damaged. Legacy endless-Senior saves migrate to a validated first-quarter policy gate, while older simulation checkpoints retain deterministic career, staffing, credit, Priority Peck, and flock-labor state without discarding incumbent hens.

## Running

Open this directory in Godot 4.7 and run the project, or use:

```powershell
& "$env:LOCALAPPDATA\Programs\Godot\4.7\Godot_v4.7-stable_win64.exe" --path .
```

Keyboard shortcuts:

- `Space`: pause or resume
- `1`, `2`, `3`: select a decision card while a briefing is open; otherwise normal, fast, and ultra speed
- `Enter`: authorize the selected policy or incident response
- `E`: perform a Priority Peck for the focused eligible hen, or the recommended eligible hen from overview
- `P`: fund a feed party
- `O`: toggle overtime
- `V`: open or close the Flockwatch Ledger
- `Click`, `Tab`, `Esc`: inspect a hen, cycle hens, or return to the office overview

## Browser Experience

The `web` directory contains a responsive frontend that mounts the Godot browser runtime directly into its game terminal.

Export the current game build:

```powershell
& "$env:LOCALAPPDATA\Programs\Godot\4.7\Godot_v4.7-stable_win64_console.exe" --headless --path . --export-release Web web/public/game/index.html
```

Then run the local frontend:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npm run dev
```

Open `http://localhost:3000`. The frontend reads the generated Godot manifest at runtime, so later game exports do not require manually updating bundle sizes.

For a repeatable responsive visual audit while the frontend is running:

```powershell
node tests/responsive_web_audit.mjs "http://localhost:3000/?build=responsive-audit" "captures/responsive-guided"
```

The audit captures 2560x1600, 1440x900, and 390x844 layouts, records canvas geometry and `render_game_to_text`, and fails when the browser reports an error.
