# Pecking Order

The current economy includes protected operating, contract, settlement, feed, land, utility, and debt reserves; visible workstation, live-file, grain-storage, publicity, and finished-egg capacity; and a strict DepartmentSimulation schema-v25 checkpoint with thirteen stable facility keys, a persisted named-manager roster, the three-parcel player-built campus, and an exact Farm Treasury journal. Thirteen complete fixed facilities are playable: the Candling & Rework Bay; the three-level Farmer Brand Packing Annex, Laying Records Annex, Farm Mutual Service Coop, Wellness Nest, Training Roost, Rooster Operations Office, IT Coop, Flock Relations Office, Flock Provisions Co-op, Harvest Credit Gallery, and Farmgate Dispatch Depot; and the Gold-gated Farm Mutual Negotiation Room. North Meadow, Orchard Row, and Creekside Yard add purchasable land, services, construction queues, finite contractor/power/cold capacity, legal module pads, named campus posts, and authored chicken commutes. The outer composite campaign-save envelope remains schema v2, while the nested Senior Roost ledger is schema v3. Purchases construct real equipment, add recurring upkeep or payroll, and change production, recovery, market access, career economics, supervision, automation, labor-case liability, feed procurement, public attribution, finished-goods settlement, or campus capacity. See [Office Expansion & Economy](docs/EXPANSION_ECONOMY.md) for the exact terms.

A darkly comic 3D management game about chickens processing insurance claims inside a corporate office. Hens peck through work and lay eggs as deliverables, roosters manage their performance, and the farmer presents the results as evidence of his leadership.

## Management Loop

Every shift opens with three morning-policy cards. The player chooses throughput, shell assurance, or sustainable flock care, reviews the complete tradeoff, and explicitly authorizes it. Each card derives a compact `ORDER FIT / WATCH` count from that day's three immutable probation orders; selecting it names the exact supported goals, conflicting goals, and longer-term file edge while stating that the closing ledger still decides the result. A new file holds Day 1 at a paused floor orientation until the player inspects a hen and chooses 1×; later briefings resume normally. At 11:00 AM and 2:00 PM, satirical office incidents auto-pause the shift and require a response; each option exposes its cost before it can be confirmed. The top HUD keeps the live egg quota, clean-clutch streak, and active policy visible throughout the shift. The existing score/day badge also carries three compact order stamps derived from the same live projection as Flockwatch; a newly safe or newly at-risk order produces one restrained visual and audio cue, while new shifts seed silently and reduced-motion mode updates without pulsing. Sound and golden eggs extend the streak and add a capped value bonus; a cracked egg resets it.

On a fresh file, the optional First Clutch coach locks onto the first inspected hen and follows only authoritative actions. It remembers valid actions completed out of order, permits wrong routing and missed Peck windows, survives Continue, and retires permanently after the target hen's assisted egg reaches the farmer or the player chooses Skip. The compact card never pauses production or replaces the selected-hen dossier.

The live queue strip separates Nest Damage, Predator Loss, and Appeals work. Selecting a hen opens her assignment dossier with specialty, current file, deadline, value, and crack risk. AUTO dispatch favors specialty and urgency; manual routing can rescue an overdue tray at the cost of a slower, riskier mismatch. Failed files return as next-shift rework, so today's shortcuts remain visible in tomorrow's workload.

When a seated hen's active claim reaches the gold section of its progress bar, the dossier exposes a Priority Peck. Pressing `E` performs a connected three-peck animation, advances only that exact claim, and changes its real quality risk. Perfect and strong timing build a chain that pays a small clean-credit bonus only if the finished egg survives grading. The limit of three assists per shift keeps it a deliberate intervention instead of another idle multiplier.

When an inspected hen approaches that window at requested 3× or 10×, **Priority Focus** temporarily runs the effective simulation at 1× while leaving the chosen speed selected. The speed control displays `3×/1×` or `10×/1×`, the header names the inspected hen and timing state, and the selected-hen dossier retains the exact rating, file-progress gain, shell-risk change, and chain result for a bounded confirmation beat. The requested speed resumes automatically. One intervention is offered per inspection so a newly pulled file cannot immediately trap the clock at 1×; selecting or cycling to a hen explicitly rearms the focus aid. Timing thresholds, rewards, charges, economy, and save authority are unchanged.

The same dossier also exposes each hen's career title, XP, work profile, trust, and grievance. Its **Support** tab names the strongest profile-fit check-in and shows the exact Feed Fund, trust, grievance, and farmer-favor consequence beside the real action buttons; its **Profile** tab keeps the hen's specialty, AUTO-routing treatment, morale, stress, fatigue, and shell risk visible without opening another management screen. Management can share basket credit, fund perch-side coaching, or authorize a stretch clutch within the day's flock-wide check-in allowance; one hen can never receive two check-ins that day. Credit improves morale and trust but costs money and farmer favor; coaching trades current speed for safer work and advancement; pressure raises output now while creating persistent quality and relationship debt. Policies, incident responses, Feed Parties, and overtime also shape the flock's memory.

On Days 2 and 4, that memory can produce a named Flock Petition in place of the second structural incident. Specialty mismatch, dangerous pace, and prior credit allocation become evidence instead of hidden modifiers. Management can sign an exact next-shift compact, buy a temporary scoop-of-feed concession, or deny and monitor. Signed promises are tested once against the authoritative closing ledger; releasing the sponsor or breaking the wording creates a breach receipt. At sufficient flock unity, denial or breach schedules one shift of work-to-rule: 18% less throughput in exchange for six points of shell safety. Flockwatch and the sponsor's dossier keep the obligation visible.

New probation files open with four occupied workstations and two applicants. During the farmer-review planning window, Flockwatch shows each candidate's specialty, work profile, career rank, one-time hire cost, and recurring wage. Management can authorize the fifth and sixth physical perches, hire into a vacant desk, or release an idle hen to pasture with a real separation package and lasting flock consequences. Hen identities and career histories survive employment changes instead of resetting into interchangeable units.

Completed eggs pause at the physical sorter for grading. The machine issues a quality/value receipt, then the egg continues along the presentation rail. Before a Farmgate Depot is commissioned, that presentation credits the Feed Fund as before. Once the Depot is operating, sound and golden eggs instead enter immutable, named-worker FIFO lots and do not become cash until the closing dispatch settles; cracked eggs remain immediate loss-bearing output. The same real egg still settles into the basket or cart and remains visible by quality. This makes the satire mechanically legible: the hen creates and signs the value, while management controls when, where, and under whose brand it becomes revenue.

Senior Roost is a recurring career rather than a renamed sixth probation day. Each year first freezes three Board Mandate cards from the opening office context; the universal **Standard Board Book** is always first, free, and available. The scheduler preserves the hardest unlocked tier and uses the remaining variety slot for an eligible Book the career has not yet mastered whenever one exists, so the seven-Book portfolio cannot stall behind repeat-only catalogs. Selecting one opens the normal first-quarter capital-policy gate. Every quarter then lasts three shifts and begins with one irreversible capital policy: concentrate $12 in Merit Grants for the top hen, spend $24 on a flock-wide dividend that gives every employed hen +34 morale, removes 34 stress and fatigue, and costs four farmer favor, or book $60 of future harvest now for farmer favor while accepting quota pressure and relationship debt. Each card names its exact score edge, score watch, and fit with the filed annual book; later-quarter reward receipts also show the current Board targets-met count and largest remaining annual blocker. Output, shell integrity, queues, welfare, compliance, farmer favor, and solvency produce an exact 100-point quarter score and up to three lifetime Roost Marks. At each closed-quarter gate, management may bank those marks or invest three marks plus the current protected Feed Fund sponsorship cost in one Accredited Layer. Without a Training Roost, her next shift with real claim progress runs 15% slower and costs $12; installed tiers disclose and reduce both terms. Afterward she permanently receives specialist speed and shell-risk treatment in one alternate lane, a torso credential, and a $1 daily wage increase. Baseline AUTO still prefers her primary lane; IT Coop level one or higher also recognizes the earned secondary credential inside its deadline-grace window, while every manual tray remains an explicit override.

The selected Board Mandate records one compact evidence row per accepted Senior shift and shows live objective values, the next unmet threshold, and the largest recoverable blocker in the Career Forecast. Each three-shift quarter files a checkpoint; twelve shifts settle the mandate exactly once at annual review. The three tier-zero books risk no marks and award one Board Seal on success. At 1/3/6 seals, harder tiers unlock with 2/4/6-mark stakes and 2/3/4-seal rewards. A successful advanced mandate returns its exact stake; failure permanently spends it. Four quarters still close the ordinary annual career review: passing awards bonus marks and raises next year's baseline quota by one, while failure opens a performance-improvement year with a two-egg increase and reduced farmer favor. Continue begins another uncapped year at a fresh three-card mandate gate.

During an active Senior quarter, Flockwatch also shows an `IF FILED NOW` Career Forecast from that same authoritative score breakdown. It reports projected score and marks, the next exact 40/60/80 threshold, and the largest recoverable scoring cause. The forecast is hidden outside active Senior play and never changes the save or pre-counts an unfinished shift.

That second review now opens with an exact score receipt instead of an unexplained total. Probation orders, daily clutch, shell quality, queue control, and flock safeguards each show their signed contribution; score caps and the shift-two specialization appear as separate reconciliation lines when applicable. Beside the filed management memo, a factual hen file preserves one character-scale moment from the shift: pressured cracked work, a golden deliverable, dangerous strain, invisible labor, or the closing ledger leader. The simulation freezes that story before daily stats and overnight strain reset, so its identity, output, relationships, and satirical copy all describe the same completed shift.

After Day 4, the farmer files a Flock Restructuring order against the bottom hen in the frozen Pecking Order. The dossier includes the ranking and the context its score omitted: specialty mismatch, career history, trust, grievance, strain, and management pressure. The player can nominate her as the variance, pay to redeploy her into her specialty, or contest the ranking with the whole flock. That choice is persisted and names the campaign ending: Farmer's Favorite, Benevolent Rooster, or The Flock Has a Voice.

Campaign progress is saved as versioned JSON after meaningful management actions, including policy and incident resolutions, petitions, compacts, work-to-rule, personnel check-ins, routing changes, hiring, releases, perch authorizations, facility purchases and capital-plan pins, land deeds, service connections, pod placement or relocation, portfolio construction and staffing, feed orders, Farmgate mandates, public campaigns or skips, Feed Parties, overtime changes, Priority Pecks, credited eggs, restructuring, completed shifts, Senior Board Mandates, capital filings, Career Sponsorships, scored quarters, and annual transitions. `Continue` restores the immutable probation ledger, the separate Senior career ledger, pending or completed cross-training, finished-egg lots and frozen dispatch terms, the pinned capital plan and exact commissioning history, all three campus parcels, their two nested ledgers, and named posts, the Farm Treasury receipt chain, the frozen Gallery evidence and receipt, the case-docket identity and exact remaining incident rotation, and the exact live office checkpoint; a verified backup is used if the primary save is damaged. ## Browser Experience

The `web` directory contains a responsive frontend that mounts the Godot browser runtime directly into its game terminal. Its Vinext toolchain requires Node.js `>=22.13.0`.

Export the current game build:

```powershell
& "$env:LOCALAPPDATA\Programs\Godot\4.7\Godot_v4.7-stable_win64_console.exe" --headless --path . --export-release Web web/public/game/index.html
```

Then run the local frontend:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npm run dev
```

To verify the shell copy and quiet accessible operations narration:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npm run lint
npm test
```

For the release soak, install Chromium once and keep the local frontend running:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npx playwright install chromium
npm run audit:soak -- http://localhost:3000/?build=runtime-soak
```

The default soak performs four real camera, Flockwatch, Settings, checkpoint,
backup-download, validated restore, and Continue cycles. It fails on browser
errors, lost authoritative state, checkpoint failures, input stalls, diagnostic
growth, WebAssembly or JavaScript heap growth beyond budget, or accumulating
Godot/DOM objects, nodes, or event listeners. Duration and budgets can be
overridden with the documented `RUNTIME_SOAK_*` environment variables in
`web/tests/runtime-soak-audit.mjs`.

For the active-production progression gate, keep the frontend running and execute:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npm run audit:progression -- http://localhost:3000/?build=active-progression
```

The default gate completes two full 10x shifts through the real campaign UI. It
handles morning directives, timed incidents, farmer reviews, closing-credit
decisions, probation reports, the first milestone, and the Farm Mutual board;
then it compares matched running Day 1 and Day 3 samples. Frozen shift evidence
must contain real eggs and quota results, checkpoints must remain verified, and
Godot/DOM/JavaScript/WebAssembly growth must stay inside the documented
`ACTIVE_PROGRESSION_*` budgets. The headless result uses ANGLE SwiftShader, so
absolute GPU throughput is reported rather than claimed as physical-hardware proof.

For the complete five-shift probation endurance gate, run the cross-platform preset:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npm run audit:probation -- http://localhost:3000/?build=full-probation
```

This follows the same production UI through all five farmer reviews and the
authored pass/fail screen. It requires a final five-shift safeguard receipt,
advancing authoritative revisions, verified checkpoints, responsive frame
presentation, bounded staffed-model growth, and zero browser errors. A valid
failed probation is recorded as an honest terminal outcome rather than treated
as a timeout; the exact failed criteria remain in the audit report for balance
analysis. `ACTIVE_PROGRESSION_*` environment variables can still override the
preset when investigating a specific renderer or machine.

For the touch-first phone audit, keep the frontend running and execute:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npm run audit:touch -- http://localhost:3000/?build=touch-first-audit
```

This starts a new campaign through touch, performs a one-finger camera pan,
uses the dedicated touch zoom control, opens and closes Flockwatch and Settings,
rotates between 844x390 landscape and 390x844 portrait, captures both layouts,
and fails on browser errors, horizontal overflow, undersized targets, a visible
handbook during focused play, or camera input leaking behind a management panel.

For the configurable-camera gate, keep the frontend running and execute:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npm run audit:camera-controls -- http://localhost:3000/?build=camera-controls-audit
```

The audit completes the real opening briefing, retires the optional coach,
opens Settings, verifies all 17 semantic actions, changes Pan Right from its
`D / Right Arrow` defaults to `J`, and returns to the floor. It fails unless the
new binding is transactionally saved, `D` is inert, `J` reaches the camera, and
the browser remains error-free. Captures include the complete scrolled camera
binding grid and the resumed office.

For the color-vision accessibility gate, keep the frontend running and execute:

```powershell
Set-Location web
npm run audit:color-vision -- http://localhost:3000/?build=color-vision-audit
```

The audit selects the safe palette through the real keyboard-accessible Settings
control, captures the `[N]`, `[P]`, and `[A]` routing cues on the active floor,
reloads the browser, and fails unless the independent preference file restores
the exact mode without browser errors.

Open `http://localhost:3000`. The frontend reads the generated Godot manifest at runtime, so later game exports do not require manually updating bundle sizes.
